<p><p>Greenhouse environment control systems encompass various market types, including hydroponics, aeroponics, aquaculture, and aquaponics. Hydroponics involves growing plants in nutrient-rich water solutions, eliminating soil and enhancing growth efficiency. Aeroponics utilizes mist or aerosol to deliver nutrients and water directly to plant roots, promoting faster growth. Aquaculture refers to raising fish and other aquatic organisms in controlled environments, while aquaponics combines aquaculture and hydroponics, creating a symbiotic system where fish waste fertilizes plants, and plants help purify the water, optimizing resource use.</p></p>
