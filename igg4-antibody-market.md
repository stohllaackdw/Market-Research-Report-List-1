<p><p>The IgG4 antibody market comprises two primary types: monoclonal antibodies and polyclonal antibodies. Monoclonal antibodies are engineered to target a specific antigen, providing high specificity and consistent performance, often used in diagnostics and therapeutics. In contrast, polyclonal antibodies are derived from multiple cell lines and can recognize various epitopes on an antigen, offering broader reactivity. This diversity makes polyclonal antibodies suitable for applications like research and immunoassays, while monoclonal antibodies are preferred for targeted treatments.</p></p>
