<p><p>Bioelectrochemical systems (BES) encompass technologies like Microbial Fuel Cells (MFCs) and Microbial Electrolysis Cells (MECs). MFCs convert organic matter directly into electrical energy using microbes, often utilized for wastewater treatment and energy recovery. In contrast, MECs utilize microorganisms to produce hydrogen gas from organic substrates by applying an external voltage, facilitating biogas production. Both systems leverage microbial metabolism for energy generation and environmental remediation, with applications in renewable energy, waste management, and sustainable resource recovery.</p></p>
