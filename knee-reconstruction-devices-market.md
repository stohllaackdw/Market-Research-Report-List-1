<p><p>Knee reconstruction devices encompass primary implants and revision implants. Primary implants, which can be cemented or cementless, are used for initial knee replacements, where cemented devices rely on bone cement for stability, while cementless options promote bone growth for secure integration. Revision implants are designed for patients requiring replacement of failed or worn-out primary implants, addressing complications like loosening or infection. Both types aim to restore functionality, alleviate pain, and enhance the quality of life for individuals with knee impairments.</p></p>
