<p><strong>The DNA Sequencing Instruments Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Emulsion PCR</li><li>Bridge Amplification</li><li>Single-molecule</li></ul></p>
<p><p>DNA sequencing instruments operate using various amplification techniques. Emulsion PCR involves encapsulating DNA fragments within microscopic droplets, enabling simultaneous amplification of multiple samples in a water-in-oil emulsion. Bridge amplification, commonly used in sequencing, involves attaching DNA fragments to a surface, where they undergo repeated cycles of amplification, forming dense clusters. Single-molecule sequencing, on the other hand, analyzes individual DNA molecules without amplification, allowing for real-time observation and reduced errors, thus enhancing accuracy and speed in genomic analysis.</p></p>
